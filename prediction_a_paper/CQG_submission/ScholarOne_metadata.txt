--- Title (复制下面一行) ---
Dimensional Selection in Finite Causal-Set Link Dynamics: Evidence for an Asymmetric 4→5 Barrier

--- Abstract (纯文本, 197 words, 复制以下段落) ---
We study dimensional selection in finite ensembles of Lorentzian-like causal sets sprinkled into flat spacetimes of dimension two through five. A link-based causal action exhibits a coupling window in which four-dimensional families consistently outscore all others, whereas the standard four-dimensional Benincasa-Dowker-Glaser action systematically selects five dimensions at every coupling tested. Component analysis traces this failure to the higher-order interval corrections, which preferentially favor causally sparse, high-dimensional configurations. The present work concerns finite causal-set ensembles rather than a controlled continuum limit.

We introduce a dimensionless diagnostic that measures the link-penalty separation per unit entropy separation between adjacent-dimensional families. This ratio is approximately ten at the four-to-five boundary, stable across three generator geometries and seven system sizes up to 112 elements, and substantially larger than the corresponding lower-dimensional thresholds. A geometrically motivated semi-analytic derivation from Minkowski light-cone geometry reproduces the numerical median to within one per cent. Blind extrapolation to six and seven dimensions yields qualitative agreement with independent numerical measurements, supporting the picture that inter-dimensional barriers intensify beyond four dimensions. The resulting asymmetric barrier structure makes four-dimensionality a favored regime of the entropy-connectivity competition: it is not four dimensions itself that is special, but the barrier above it.
