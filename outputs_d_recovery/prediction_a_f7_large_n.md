## 4. Structural Opposition Quantification

The core conflict: Mechanism I needs wall(2D/3D) >> wall(4D),
but Mechanism II needs logH(4D) << logH(5D).

| N | ΔlogH(4D-2D) | ΔlogH(4D-3D) | ΔlogH(5D-4D) | Δwall(4D-2D) | Δwall(4D-3D) | Δwall(5D-4D) |
|---|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 20 | +11.5 | +4.4 | +2.6 | -14.31 | -10.82 | -1.62 |
| 36 | +25.7 | +8.6 | +6.0 | -10.20 | -10.19 | -1.72 |
| 52 | +40.4 | +13.6 | +9.5 | -4.17 | -4.17 | -5.75 |
| 72 | +63.9 | +23.1 | +13.5 | -2.35 | -2.35 | -6.08 |
| 100 | +93.6 | +32.7 | +21.4 | -0.04 | -0.04 | -7.06 |

**Diagnosis**: ΔlogH(4D−2D) grows O(N) while Δwall(4D−2D) → 0.
ΔlogH(5D−4D) also grows O(N) but starts positive — this is what makes 4D<5D work.
