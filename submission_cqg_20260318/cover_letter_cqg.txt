To: The Editors, Classical and Quantum Gravity

Re: Submission of manuscript "Geometric Phase Transitions and Emergent Dimensional Selection in Finite Causal Poset Ensembles"

Dear Editors,

I would like to submit the enclosed manuscript for consideration in Classical and Quantum Gravity.

The manuscript studies whether geometric, Lorentzian-like causal order can emerge as a competitive phase in finite causal-poset ensembles despite the Kleitman-Rothschild entropy dominance. Using exact linear extension counts (exact combinatorial entropy) for N = 10-44, we show a bounded, O(1) critical coupling gamma_c for the Lorentzian-like 2D family to overtake KR-like posets under a structurally motivated action. We then show that, under a non-target-anchored dimensional consistency action, 4D Lorentzian-like candidates dominate across N = 20-72 on the scanned grid, with increasing margins versus 2D and 3D.

Key points of novelty:
- Exact-entropy phase-transition evidence across a wide confirmatory range (N = 10-44) with an O(1) threshold.
- Systematic ablation identifies a minimal action backbone and replaces the target-anchored dimension term by a non-target-anchored consistency term, addressing circularity concerns.
- A unified narrative connecting bounded geometric phase emergence and dimensional selection within the same finite poset ensemble framework.

Code and configuration files needed to reproduce the reported results are organized in the accompanying repository under "理论体系/poset_phase" and in the associated archived releases cited in the manuscript.

This work is not under consideration elsewhere. I have no conflicts of interest to declare.
