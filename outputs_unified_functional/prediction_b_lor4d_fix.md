## 6. Physical Interpretation

### Why logH penalizes the right structure

- Lor4D has **low R** (few causal pairs relative to total) → weak ordering → high logH
- KR has **moderate R** → more ordering → lower logH
- The gap ΔlogH ~ +0.18N grows linearly, overwhelming any bounded wall

### F8d interpretation: disorder surplus correction

- logH - μN(1-R) subtracts the 'expected excess entropy' from low R
- Physical meaning: structures shouldn't be penalized for having
  the *right* causal structure (few pairs = high-d Lorentzian)
- μ captures the rate at which entropy grows per unit of 'missing' ordering

### F8b interpretation: extensive wall

- β·N·σ((R-Rc)/w) is an N-extensive admissibility term
- In the continuum limit, this corresponds to an action density
  (energy per volume) rather than a finite-size correction
- Natural form: S_EH is extensive (∝ N), so the wall should also be extensive
