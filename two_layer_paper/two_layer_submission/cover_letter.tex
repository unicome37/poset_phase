Please consider our manuscript, \emph{Layered Structural Screening of 4D
Lorentzian Causal Sets: Linear Admissibility, Quadratic Identity, and
Historical Sedimentation}, for publication in \emph{Physical Review D}.

The paper presents a numerical study of finite causal sets based on two
functionally distinct screening layers. The first layer, a linear
Benincasa--Dowker admissibility gate, removes non-geometric posets but does not
uniquely identify 4D Lorentzian causal sets. The second layer, a quadratic
reference-manifold distance in a three-observable feature space, separates the
4D Lorentzian class robustly within the tested family library.

We believe the manuscript will be of interest to readers working on causal set
theory, discrete gravity, and the emergence of continuum geometry from finite
causal structure.
