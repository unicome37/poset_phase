Please consider our manuscript, ``Curvature Encoding in Finite Causal Sets:
Purely Causal Observables Across de~Sitter, Schwarzschild, and FLRW
Backgrounds,'' for publication in \emph{Physical Review D}.

The paper presents numerical evidence that finite causal sets encode
background spacetime curvature through observables constructed solely from the
partial order. Across de~Sitter, Schwarzschild, and FLRW backgrounds, we find
that the occupancy ratio exhibits a gate-like response to curvature, the
antichain-based channel tracks the Hubble parameter more robustly than the
spectral channel, and a calibrated two-step construction provides a
high-fidelity bridge to scalar curvature at the largest finite sizes
considered.

We believe the manuscript will be of interest to readers working on causal set
theory, discrete gravity, and emergent spacetime.
