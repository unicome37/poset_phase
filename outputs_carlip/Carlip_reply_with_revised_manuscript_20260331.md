Dear Professor Carlip,

Thank you for your very careful reading and for taking the time to write such a direct critique. I went back through the draft, the citations, and the underlying literature line by line, and you were right that the original version was not careful enough.

In particular, I have corrected the inaccurate descriptions of your paper, Surya’s review, and Loomis and Carlip’s work. The passages that referred to midpoint scaling, a formal CST partition function framework, and Monte Carlo simulations were not faithful to the sources, and they have been removed or rewritten.

I also revisited the broader literature on phase transitions in causal sets and updated the framing accordingly. The revised manuscript now cites the relevant combinatorial and phase-transition background, including the Kleitman-Rothschild result, Dhar’s work on the entropy function, and Prömel et al. on the fuller phase structure. More importantly, I now present the project as a finite structural/combinatorial analysis rather than as a claim that the original log-extension functional directly captures physical entropy.

The revised version also broadens the comparison class beyond the original narrow family set, so the discussion is no longer limited to a small hand-selected sample. I have tried to separate more carefully between:
- combinatorial complexity proxies,
- finite-library structural selection,
- and any physical interpretation that would require additional justification.

At the current stage, I would summarize the revised claim much more modestly: the work provides finite-size evidence for a structural selection pattern inside the tested library, but it does not claim a full physical derivation or an asymptotic theorem.

I am attaching the revised manuscript here. If you are willing, I would be grateful for any comment on whether this revised framing is now closer to the relevant literature on causal-set phase structure and whether there are any remaining conceptual mistakes I should fix before taking it further.
