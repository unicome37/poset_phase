  Fix: use w*(N) = μ_w(N) (empirical centroid) instead of fitted formula.

FINAL SUMMARY
========================================================================

The gradient sign-flip is caused by a SINGLE mechanism:

  1. ∇S_BD always has positive width component (BDG → wider is lower action)
  2. ∇F_LSD width component = 10(w_centroid - w*(N))
     - At small N: w_centroid > w*(N) → positive → ALIGNED with ∇S_BD
     - At large N: w_centroid < w*(N) → negative → ANTI-ALIGNED
  3. The crossover occurs because:
     - w_centroid(∞) converges to ~0.21-0.25 (physical limit)
     - w*(∞) = 0.3255 (fitted well center, too high for large N)

Implications:
  - The sign flip is NOT a bug in S_BD or LSD-Well individually
  - It reveals that the w*(N) scaling law diverges from the
    actual Lor4D trajectory at large N
  - Fix: replace w*(N) = 0.3255 + 3.80/N with w*(N) = μ_w(N)
    or equivalently refit w* to track the Lor4D centroid
  - The BDG–LSD structural alignment is ROBUST once the
    trivial well-center sign convention is factored out
