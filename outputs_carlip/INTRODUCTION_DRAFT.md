## §1. The Entropy Catastrophe

The causal set programme posits that spacetime is fundamentally a locally finite partial order (poset), with the causal relation encoding both topology and geometry [Bombelli *et al.* 1987]. A central challenge emerges at the statistical level: the Kleitman-Rothschild theorem [Kleitman & Rothschild 1975] proves that almost all $n$-element posets are three-layered structures with a $(n/4,\, n/2,\, n/4)$ partition, bearing no resemblance to any Lorentzian manifold. In the discrete path integral $Z = \sum_{\mathcal{C}} e^{iS[\mathcal{C}]}$, the entropy of such non-geometric posets overwhelms the Boltzmann weight of manifold-like causal sets by an exponential factor. This is the **entropy catastrophe**: without an additional selection mechanism, the causal set path integral is dominated by configurations that have nothing to do with spacetime.

Dhar [1978] mapped the structure of generic finite posets to a lattice gas with three-body interactions and showed that the occupation-fraction $\rho$ (the fraction of comparable pairs) undergoes a first-order phase transition. Prömel, Steger & Taraz [2001] extended this to a complete phase diagram, demonstrating infinitely many transitions as $\rho$ increases—from the KR three-layer regime ($\rho \sim 1/4$) through two-, four-, and higher-layer phases. These results establish that finite posets have a rich combinatorial phase structure, but leave open the question of how a *physical* selection mechanism could navigate this landscape to pick out the manifold-like sector.

## §2. The Benincasa-Dowker Action and Its Limitations

Benincasa & Dowker [2010] constructed a discrete analogue of the Einstein-Hilbert action for causal sets. In four dimensions, the action takes the form

$$S_{\mathrm{BD}}^{(4)} = N - C_0 + 9C_1 - 16C_2 + 8C_3,$$

where $C_k$ denotes the number of order-intervals of length $k$. The coefficients are uniquely determined by the requirement that $S_{\mathrm{BD}}$ reproduces the scalar curvature integral in the continuum limit [Benincasa & Dowker 2010]. This makes $S_{\mathrm{BD}}$ a *linear* functional of the interval counts $\{C_k\}$.

Loomis & Carlip [2018] demonstrated that the BD action and related link actions do suppress certain classes of non-manifoldlike causal sets relative to Lorentzian ones. However, as we shall show, this suppression is incomplete: $S_{\mathrm{BD}}$ alone ranks 4D Lorentzian causal sets (Lor4D) only 14th out of 17 families at $N=128$. The action functions as an *admissibility gate*—filtering out posets with grossly incorrect average curvature—but cannot uniquely select the target geometry.

## §3. Structural Feature Space and the Selection Problem

To go beyond linear observables, we work in a three-dimensional structural feature space with coordinates

$$\mathbf{I}(P) = \bigl(d_{\mathrm{eff}}(P),\; C_1(P)/C_0(P),\; w(P)/N\bigr),$$

where:
- $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is the Myrheim-Meyer effective dimension [Myrheim 1978; Meyer 1988], computed from the fraction of comparable pairs via the inversion $d_{\mathrm{eff}} = f_2^{-1}(C_0/\binom{N}{2})$;
- $C_1/C_0$ is the interval fraction, encoding the ratio of length-1 to length-0 intervals and closely related to the link density [Mathur, Singh & Surya 2020];
- $w/N$ is the normalized maximum antichain width, a proxy for the "spatial extent" of the poset.

For a 4D Lorentzian causal set generated by sprinkling into a causal diamond, each feature has a well-defined expectation. The Myrheim-Meyer formula gives $\langle d_{\mathrm{eff}} \rangle \to 4.0$; the interval fraction converges to a value derivable from a Beta$(2,2)$ integral framework; and the maximum antichain width scales as $w \propto N^{1-1/d}$ with $1/d \approx 0.25$ in $d=4$ [Brightwell & Georgiou 2010]. As $N$ grows, these expectations converge to a limiting point $\boldsymbol{\mu}(\infty)$, and the fluctuations about this point shrink as $\sigma^2 \propto N^{-1}$ (standard CLT scaling).

## §4. The Two-Layer Screening Architecture

The central finding of this paper is that 4D Lorentzian causal sets are selected from a broad poset library by a **two-layer screening architecture**:

**Layer 1 — Linear admissibility** ($S_{\mathrm{BD}}$): The BD action defines a hyperplane in interval-count space. Posets whose average curvature content deviates grossly from the 4D Lorentzian value are assigned large $|S_{\mathrm{BD}}|$ and are eliminated. This layer is necessary but not sufficient.

**Layer 2 — Quadratic identity** ($S_{\mathrm{MD}}$): The minimum-distortion action

$$S_{\mathrm{MD}}[P,N] = \bigl(\mathbf{I}(P) - \boldsymbol{\mu}(N)\bigr)^\top \Sigma^{-1}(N) \bigl(\mathbf{I}(P) - \boldsymbol{\mu}(N)\bigr)$$

measures the Mahalanobis distance from the Lor4D reference manifold $\boldsymbol{\mu}(N)$ in feature space, weighted by the inverse covariance $\Sigma^{-1}(N)$ of the Lor4D ensemble. This quadratic functional has **zero free parameters**: the center and metric are entirely determined by the Lor4D ensemble statistics. It uniquely ranks Lor4D first across all tested scales ($N=16$–$1024$) and all 25 poset families, including 8 adversarial constructions designed to challenge the ranking.

The two layers are nearly orthogonal: the Pearson correlation between $S_{\mathrm{BD}}$ and $S_{\mathrm{MD}}$ ranges from $0.57$ ($N=16$) to $-0.26$ ($N=256$), approaching zero as $N$ grows. Their intersection is Lor4D and only Lor4D.

## §5. Basin Deepening and the Continuum Limit

As the poset size $N$ increases, the Lor4D identity basin deepens monotonically. We quantify this via:

- The **Mahalanobis gap** (difference between Lor4D and the nearest competitor): $-0.8$ at $N=16$, growing to $94.1$ at $N=128$ and $1.93\times10^8$ at $N=1024$;
- The **effective potential depth**: $V_{\mathrm{eff}} \propto N^{-1.57}$;
- The **Fisher information**: $\propto N^{1.00}$, implying that the statistical cost of misidentification grows linearly with $N$;
- The **reference manifold covariance collapse**: $\det(\Sigma) \propto N^{-3.38}$, so the Lor4D point-cloud volume shrinks as $N^{-1.69}$.

These scalings suggest that in the continuum limit $N \to \infty$, the Lor4D reference manifold concentrates to a point, and the Mahalanobis separation diverges—making 4D Lorentzian geometry the unique attractor in feature space.

## §6. Scope and Outline

The present work is strictly numerical: we do not claim analytic proofs, but rather a systematic computational survey across a large and diverse poset library. Our sample space includes:

- **Geometric families**: 4D Lorentzian (Lor4D), 2D, 3D, and 5D Lorentzian causal sets sprinkled into causal diamonds;
- **KR-type families**: 2-layer, 4-layer, and 8-layer random bipartite/tripartite posets approximating the Kleitman-Rothschild generic structure;
- **Random layered structures**: $k$-layer random DAGs with varying density;
- **Percolation/interval orders**: Bernoulli random partial orders, interval orders;
- **Adversarial families**: 8 constructions specifically designed to mimic one or more features of Lor4D (chain-antichain hybrids, sparse chains, mixed Lor4D with noise, etc.).

The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 defines the poset families and the three structural features. Section 3 presents the LSD-Well functional and its zero-parameter Mahalanobis variant. Section 4 derives the two-layer screening structure. Section 5 quantifies basin deepening and $N$-scaling. Section 6 discusses the order-raising correlation between the two layers and the reference manifold as a theoretical object. Section 7 concludes.
