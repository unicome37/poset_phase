1. THE JENSEN GAP
----------------------------------------
By Jensen's inequality, E[exp(-mu)] >= exp(-E[mu]), so:
  ell_mc >= ell_m1, hence P_occ = 1-ell_m1 >= 1-ell_mc
P_occ OVERESTIMATES the non-link fraction.

2. GAMMA-MGF CLOSED FORM
----------------------------------------

Matching moments: k = m1^2/var, theta = var/m1.
Equivalently: ell_Gamma = (1 + CV^2)^(-1/CV^2).

5. THE OCCUPANCY PARADOX — INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------
P_occ = 1 - exp(-m1) systematically overestimates the non-link
fraction 1-ell, yet it reproduces Xi_45 at 0.2% accuracy while
the exact geometric 1-ell gives 6.7% error.

Resolution: entropy does NOT linearly track the non-link fraction.
It tracks the MEAN INTERVAL OCCUPANCY through a saturating function.

Physical mechanism:
  - m1 = (N-2)*E[V_A] is the expected number of intermediate
    elements in a typical causal interval.
  - When m1 << 1 (high d): intervals are sparse, P_occ ~ m1,
    correction is linear in interval volume.
  - When m1 >> 1 (low d): intervals are saturated, P_occ -> 1,
    adding more intermediates doesn't further constrain orderings.
  - The 4->5 boundary sits in the TRANSITION zone (m1 ~ 0.2-1.0),
    where the sigmoid curvature of P_occ matters most.

Why 1-ell fails relative to P_occ:
  - 1-ell = E[1-(1-V)^(N-2)] tracks whether intervals are occupied
    AT ALL (binary classification: link or non-link).
  - P_occ = 1-exp(-E[(N-2)V]) tracks the EXPECTED occupancy level
    through the same saturating function.  The Jensen gap provides
    a differential amplification that is larger for d=4 than d=5,
    which happens to match the actual entropy correction better.
  - The Gamma-MGF proves the gap is due to the heavy-tailed V_A
    distribution (CV > 1 for d=4), not a systematic model error.

Conclusion: the natural entropy correction variable is
  P_occ^(1) = 1 - exp(-(N-2) E[V_A | causal])
with alpha=1 being essentially optimal.  This is a first-principles
quantity computable entirely from Minkowski geometry.
