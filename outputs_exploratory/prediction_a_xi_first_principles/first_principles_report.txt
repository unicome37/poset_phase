Model assumptions

  2. Link probability uses the flat Alexandrov volume emptiness factor:
       P_link(Δ) ~= (1 - kappa_d * tau^d)^(N-2).
  3. Entropy uses the mean-field closure:
       logH/N ~= (1 - p_d) * (log N - 1).

Interpretation
  - The numerator is now geometry-driven: no empirical C0/N fit is used.
  - The denominator is controlled by p_d alone; the 4->5 entropy gap shrinks
    because p_d already becomes small by d=4.
  - The raw mean-field closure is too stiff; a single global renormalization
    already absorbs most of the finite-generator mismatch.
  - Replacing the additive intercept by link sparsity produces an even tighter
    geometry-side closure, suggesting that the missing entropy correction tracks
    the fraction of causal relations that are still non-link mediated.
