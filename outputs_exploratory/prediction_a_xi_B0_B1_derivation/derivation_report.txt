H. PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION
----------------------------------------

  The entropy has a one-parameter decomposition:
    h = h₀ − ε·(h₀ − 2·P_occ)
    = (1−ε)·h₀ + 2ε·P_occ

  where (h₀ − 2·P_occ) is the 'correctable entropy surplus'.

  Physical meaning of ε:
  ε ≈ 0.066 represents the fraction of mean-field entropy
  that is 'transferred' from the incomparable-pair channel
  to the interval-occupancy channel by transitive correlations.

  When the correctable surplus is positive (high d: h₀ >> 2P_occ),
  the correction REDUCES entropy (mean-field overcounts).
  When it is negative (low d: h₀ < 2P_occ, i.e., d=2),
  the correction INCREASES entropy (mean-field undercounts).

  The factor 2 in '2·P_occ' arises because each occupied interval
  affects TWO elements (the endpoints), each gaining ε units of
  'entropy restitution' from the chain internal structure.

  If ε = κ₄/2 = π/48, this connects the entropy transfer to
  the 4-dimensional Alexandrov volume constant, suggesting that
  the correction is anchored to the d=4 causal geometry — the
  dimension where the Ξ barrier naturally emerges.
