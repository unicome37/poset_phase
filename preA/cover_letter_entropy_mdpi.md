Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit the enclosed manuscript for consideration in *Entropy*. This paper is a companion to our concurrently submitted work "Bounded Geometric Phase Transition in Finite Causal Posets with Exact Entropy" (Prediction B), which establishes the existence of a bounded geometric phase transition in finite causal posets and introduces a non-target-anchored consistency penalty as a replacement for dimension-specific priors.

**Motivation and scope fit.** This paper tests a direct prediction arising from the companion work: if the geometric action rewards dimensional *consistency* rather than proximity to a specific target dimension, then higher-dimensional Lorentzian-like structures should naturally dominate lower-dimensional ones. The central objects are combinatorial entropy $H(P) = \log |L(P)|$ of finite posets and the competition between entropy maximization and structural consistency penalties — a free energy competition that falls squarely within *Entropy's* scope on information-theoretic and statistical-mechanical foundations.

**Summary of contributions.**

1. **Prediction test.** We extend the poset ensemble to include a 4D Lorentzian-like family and test across $N = 20$–$72$ (14 sizes, 4 families, 3 action variants). Under the consistency-based action, 4D posets achieve unconditional dominance (92/98 configurations), while under the original target-anchored action, 4D and KR posets are locked in a near-perfect tie (36 vs 35 wins).

2. **Growing margin.** The margin of victory for 4D over 2D posets grows monotonically from +7 at $N = 20$ to +57 at $N = 72$ under the consistency action, suggesting the dominance strengthens at larger $N$.

3. **Seed robustness.** At $N = 72$, three independent generator seeds confirm 100% win rate for 4D posets under consistency variants (21/21 configurations), versus 43% under the original action.

4. **Diagnostic signature.** The near-perfect KR–lor4d tie under the original action provides a diagnostic signature: the target-anchored penalty creates a dimension-specific "glass ceiling" that the consistency replacement removes.

**Novelty.** While the companion paper establishes the phase transition mechanism and the non-circularity of the consistency replacement, this paper demonstrates its predictive power: the consistency-based action not only preserves the transition but selects for higher-dimensional geometric coherence. This is the first numerical demonstration that dimensional consistency constraints alone, without dimension-specific priors, favor higher-dimensional Lorentzian structures in finite causal poset ensembles.
