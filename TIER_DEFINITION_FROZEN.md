# Tier-1 / Tier-2 Candidate Pool Frozen Definition
## Date: 2026-03-16  |  Status: FROZEN

### Motivation

The `prediction_b_candidate_expansion_layered` experiment (commit `5360ead`) revealed that
parameter-tunable random layered posets (`random_layered_k*`) systematically outperform
`lorentzian_like_2d` under both `A2_full` and `A2_replace_dim_with_consistency` for N ≥ 36.

This does **not** invalidate Prediction B. It exposes the **discriminative ceiling** of the
current action: all observables (width, height, cover_density, closure) are order-theoretic
and can be reverse-engineered by tuning (k, adjacent_p, skip_p, imbalance_mode).

### Structural discriminant: `aw/√N` scaling law

| Family class | `aw/√N` (mean ± std, 10 seeds × 6 N) | Scaling behavior |
|---|---|---|
| **Lor2D** | **1.370 ± 0.177** | Near-constant (~√N antichain width) |
| Layered k4–k8 | 1.71–2.05 ± 0.28–0.35 | Higher constant plateau |
| Lor3D / TP | 2.36–2.54 | Intermediate |
| MLR | 2.69 ± 0.84 | High variance |
| KR / Lor4D | 3.09–3.14 | Linearly growing with N |

**Mann-Whitney U test**: Lor2D vs all Tier-2 pooled: U = 1548, p = 1.94 × 10⁻²⁸.
Per-N tests all significant at p < 0.005.

The `aw/√N` ratio is **already computable from existing observables** and provides
a clean structural separation between geometric sprinklings and random layered constructions.
